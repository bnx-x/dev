Push Day
Chest · Shoulders · Triceps  ·  ~45–55 min
🏃 Cardio Warm-Up — 10–12 min
OPTION A
Treadmill Walk/JogStart at a brisk walk (3.5 mph), increase to a light jog (5–5.5 mph) for last 5 min. Keep it comfortable.
OPTION B
Stationary BikeEasy resistance, moderate pace. Great for days when legs feel heavy. No pressure on your back.
💪 Strength & Calisthenics
1
Push-Ups (Calisthenics)
3 Sets 8–12 Reps 60 sec rest
▾
How To Do It
  • Place hands slightly wider than shoulder-width, arms straight.
  • Keep your body in a straight line from head to heels — no sagging hips.
  • Lower your chest to about an inch from the floor, elbows at ~45°.
  • Press back up to start. Breathe out on the way up.
  • Can't do 8? Start on your knees. No shame — build from there.
Keep your head neutral — don't tuck chin or look up. Imagine a glass of water on your head.
2
Dumbbell Chest Press
3 Sets 10–12 Reps 90 sec rest
▾
How To Do It
  • Lie on a flat bench, feet flat on the floor. Hold a dumbbell in each hand at chest level.
  • Elbows should be at about 45° from your body — not flared out wide.
  • Press both dumbbells up until arms are fully extended above your chest.
  • Slowly lower back down to chest level. Control the descent.
  • Start light — 10–20 lbs each. Form over weight every time.
Rest your entire head on the bench. Don't lift your neck to look at the weights.
3
Dumbbell Shoulder Press
3 Sets 10 Reps 90 sec rest
▾
How To Do It
  • Sit on a bench with back support (use an incline bench set upright). Hold dumbbells at ear level.
  • Press both dumbbells up until arms are almost fully extended.
  • Slowly lower back to ear/jaw height.
  • Keep your core tight and back pressed into the bench.
Use a seat with back support. Press straight up — don't tilt your head back. Keep chin level.
4
Tricep Pushdowns (Cable)
3 Sets 12–15 Reps 60 sec rest
▾
How To Do It
  • Set cable to high position with a rope or bar attachment. Stand facing the machine.
  • Grip the attachment, elbows tucked to your sides at 90°.
  • Push the attachment down until your arms are fully extended. Squeeze the triceps.
  • Slowly return to the start — resist the weight on the way up.
Keep your shoulders down and relaxed — don't shrug into the movement. Stand tall.
5
Plank Hold (Core)
3 Sets 20–40 sec 60 sec rest
▾
How To Do It
  • Get on your forearms and toes. Elbows under shoulders.
  • Keep your body in a straight line — no raised butt, no sag.
  • Breathe steadily. Brace your abs like you're about to get punched.
  • Hold for time. Rest. Repeat.
Look at the floor a foot in front of you — keeps your neck neutral. Don't lift your head up.
ONE SET AT A TIME. THAT'S ALL.
You don't have to be perfect. You just have to show up.

Pull Day
Back · Biceps · Core  ·  ~45–55 min
🏃 Cardio Warm-Up — 10–12 min
OPTION A
Rowing MachineLight resistance. Great full-body warm-up that opens the back. Keep a tall posture — drive with legs, pull with arms.
OPTION B
EllipticalArms and legs together. Light effort. Good to mobilize without impacting back.
💪 Strength & Calisthenics
1
Lat Pulldown (Cable)
3 Sets 10–12 Reps 90 sec rest
▾
How To Do It
  • Sit at the lat pulldown machine, thighs secured under the pad. Grip bar just wider than shoulder width.
  • Lean back very slightly — about 10–15°. This is your position for the whole set.
  • Pull the bar down to your upper chest, driving your elbows down and back.
  • Slowly let the bar rise back up, feeling the stretch in your lats (sides of back).
Don't pull the bar behind your head — always pull to your upper chest. This protects your neck and shoulders.
2
Seated Cable Row
3 Sets 10–12 Reps 90 sec rest
▾
How To Do It
  • Sit at the row machine, feet on platform, knees slightly bent. Grab the handle.
  • Start with arms extended, sitting tall — slight natural arch in your lower back.
  • Pull the handle into your stomach, squeezing your shoulder blades together.
  • Slowly return arms to full extension. Don't let your back round.
Keep your chest up and shoulders BACK throughout. Don't let your upper back round or your neck jut forward.
3
Assisted Pull-Ups or Dead Hangs
3 Sets 5–8 Reps / 20 sec hang 90 sec rest
▾
How To Do It
  • Use the assisted pull-up machine if available — set the counterweight so you can do 5–8 reps.
  • If no machine: just hang from the bar for 20 seconds. This builds grip and decompresses your spine.
  • For pull-ups: pull until chin clears the bar, then slowly lower yourself down.
  • Aim to use less counterweight each week as you get stronger.
Dead hangs actually relieve neck and upper back tension. Let gravity decompress your spine. Breathe and relax your shoulders fully.
4
Dumbbell Bicep Curls
3 Sets 12 Reps each 60 sec rest
▾
How To Do It
  • Stand or sit. Hold a dumbbell in each hand, palms facing forward, arms at your sides.
  • Curl both dumbbells up toward your shoulders. Keep elbows at your sides — don't swing.
  • Squeeze at the top, then slowly lower back down.
  • Alternate or do both together — either works. Go slow on the way down.
Keep your shoulders relaxed and still. If you're swinging your body to lift, the weight is too heavy. Drop down and go lighter.
5
Dead Bug (Core)
3 Sets 8 Reps each side 60 sec rest
▾
How To Do It
  • Lie flat on your back. Arms pointing straight up toward the ceiling. Legs lifted, knees bent at 90°.
  • Press your lower back into the floor and keep it there throughout.
  • Slowly lower your right arm overhead AND your left leg toward the floor — but don't touch.
  • Bring them back up. Repeat on the other side. That's one rep.
Your head stays flat on the floor the entire time. This is one of the best exercises for people with neck and back issues — no stress on the spine.
PROGRESS, NOT PERFECTION.
Every rep you do today is a vote for the person you're becoming.

Legs Day
Quads · Hamstrings · Glutes · Calves  ·  ~50–60 min
🏃 Cardio Warm-Up — 10 min
OPTION A
Stationary BikeLow resistance, comfortable pace. Warms up your knees and hips before squats. Ideal for leg day.
OPTION B
Incline Treadmill WalkSet incline to 4–6. Walk at a brisk pace. Activates glutes and hamstrings before your workout.
💪 Strength & Calisthenics
1
Goblet Squat (Dumbbell)
3 Sets 12 Reps 90 sec rest
▾
How To Do It
  • Hold one dumbbell vertically at your chest with both hands — like holding a goblet.
  • Stand with feet shoulder-width apart, toes slightly turned out.
  • Squat down — chest up, knees tracking over your toes, weight in your heels.
  • Go until thighs are parallel to the floor (or as low as comfortable). Drive back up.
Keep your chest up and eyes forward — this naturally protects your upper back. Don't look down at your feet.
2
Romanian Deadlift (Dumbbells)
3 Sets 10 Reps 90 sec rest
▾
How To Do It
  • Hold a dumbbell in each hand in front of your thighs. Stand tall, soft bend in knees.
  • Hinge at your hips — push your butt backward as you lower the dumbbells along your legs.
  • Lower until you feel a stretch in your hamstrings (usually mid-shin level).
  • Drive your hips forward to stand back up. Squeeze glutes at the top.
Keep your spine neutral throughout — no rounding. Your neck is part of your spine, so if your back rounds, your neck follows. Chest up, neutral head.
3
Bodyweight Lunges
3 Sets 10 each leg 90 sec rest
▾
How To Do It
  • Stand tall. Step forward with one foot about 2–2.5 feet.
  • Lower your back knee toward the floor — stop just before it touches.
  • Your front knee should stay over your ankle, not past your toes.
  • Push through your front heel to stand back up. Alternate legs.
  • Hold dumbbells for added challenge once bodyweight feels easy.
Keep your torso upright — don't lean forward. Maintaining this posture keeps tension off your upper back and neck.
4
Leg Press (Machine)
3 Sets 12–15 Reps 90 sec rest
▾
How To Do It
  • Sit in the leg press machine, back flat against the pad. Feet shoulder-width on the platform.
  • Release the safety handles and lower the platform by bending your knees toward your chest.
  • Stop before your lower back lifts off the seat. Press back to almost full extension.
  • Don't lock out your knees at the top — keep a slight bend.
Keep your head against the headrest — don't lift your neck to check the weight. Look straight ahead at the platform.
5
Calf Raises (Bodyweight or Machine)
3 Sets 15–20 Reps 45 sec rest
▾
How To Do It
  • Stand with feet shoulder-width apart, or use the calf raise machine/step at the gym.
  • Rise up onto the balls of your feet as high as you can. Hold for a second at the top.
  • Slowly lower your heels back down — go slow on the way down for max benefit.
  • Use a step edge for extra range of motion — lower heel below the step level.
LEG DAY. DONE RIGHT.
Strong legs carry you further than motivation ever will.

REST & RECOVERY DAY

Rest days are where the gains actually happen. Your muscles repair and grow while you recover — this day matters.

💧
Hydrate All DayAim for half your bodyweight in ounces of water. If you're 180 lbs → 90 oz minimum. Add electrolytes if you sweat heavily.
🧘
Neck & Upper Back ReliefDo 5 slow neck rolls each direction. Chin tucks (chin back toward your neck, hold 5 sec). Shoulder rolls. Just 5 minutes.
🚶
Active Recovery WalkA 20–30 min gentle walk keeps blood flowing without taxing your muscles. This speeds recovery, not slows it.
🥩
Eat Enough ProteinAim for ~0.7–1g of protein per pound of bodyweight. Chicken, eggs, Greek yogurt, cottage cheese, protein shakes — all count.
😴
Sleep Is the #1 Tool7–9 hours. More important than any supplement. Sleep is when muscle actually repairs and grows. Prioritize it.
📅
Missed a Day? Just Resume.Life happens. A missed day is not a failed week. Your only job is to show up to the next scheduled session. That's it.
REST IS PART OF THE WORK.
The greats don't skip rest days. They earn them.
